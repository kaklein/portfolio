katie klein

software development student @ Bellevue University

Hobbies

Music

Katie has been immersed in music her whole life. She took 10 years of piano lessons and sang in choirs from middle school through college. She loves making music with her whole family who play various instruments (piano, trombone, clarinet, euphonium, flute, guitar, drums). In 2021, she's excited to be joining the Sing Omaha Women's Choir. Katie also dabbles in music composition and production and would love to one day write music for a video game.

Video Games

Since her older brother introduced her to Nintendo's Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time as a child, Katie has loved video games, with a particular interest in the Legend of Zelda series. She gravitates toward games with immersive worlds, well-structured stories, and a good mix of puzzles and combat. Her favorite Zelda titles are Twilight Princess, Majora's Mask, and Ocarina of Time. Katie also likes playing indie games, including two of her current favorites, Celeste and Hades.

Running

Katie has been running since 2018 when she started working for the Air Force Marathon and figured if she was going to put on a running event, she should probably start running herself. As someone who never thought of herself as an 'athlete,' she loves that running gives her an easy way to stay fit and motivated. She enjoys her solo runs, but especially loves the joy and excitement that comes with doing an official race. She has run several 5ks and 10ks as well as two half marathons and is currently training for her first full marathon in 2021.

Experience & Accomplishments

Event Administrator

During her two years in this role at the Air Force Marathon, Katie handled all of the customer service for the AFM's annual events which typically have about 12,000 participants. She was also in charge of set-up and oversight of the runner registration site/database. She loved working with a small team of 7 people and being able to see the different stages of event planning culminating in the excitement of race weekend.

BA in English

Katie received her first bachelor's degree from the University of Wisconsin - Eau Claire in 2017. She majored in English and minored in Spanish. Throughout her time there, she grew her writing and communication skills and studied abroad in Valladolid, Spain. She also took several unique honors classes including a digital game design class, which is what inspired her to pursue a second degree in software development from Bellevue University a few years after graduation.

Cats

In January 2019, Katie and her husband Adam adopted two kittens: Clara and Anton. They're named after composers Clara Schumann and Anton Bruckner due to Katie and Adam's shared love of music. While they aren't biologically related, they get along just as siblings would: play fighting and getting annoyed with each other half of the time, and cuddling and grooming each other the other half.
